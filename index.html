Welcome to Baraigram Upazila Samity

Baraigram is a Upazila of Natore District in Rajshahi Division located in northern Bangladesh. It borders the metropolitan city of Rajshahi, and used to be part of Rajshahi district.

Origin of name

At first Baraigram was a [[beel]] (a large water body) whose name was Chaivhanga. In 1706 Raja Ramjibon Rai established his capital here by filling the beel. Up until 1821 Baraigram was the central office of Rajshahi district. In 1845 Baraigram Mahakuma was established. Baraigram Municipalityy was established in 1869. Natore became a fullfledged district in 1984. “Indigo resistance” movements took place in this district during 1859 and 1860.

Geography

Most part of Baraigram Upazila is plain land. Chalan Beel, the largest beel in Bangladesh is in part of Natore district. Baraigram (Natore district) with an area of 299.61 km2, is bordered by Naogaon and Bogra districts to the north, Pabna and Kushtia districts to the south, Pabna and Sirajganj districts to the east, Rajshahi district to the west. The road distance from Natore to Dhaka is 220 kilometres. Lalpur is the lowest Average annual rainfall area of Bangladesh.
